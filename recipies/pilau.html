Pilau

Description

Pilau dish is common in India and the Middle Eastern countries, each with its way of preparing it. The East African pilau rice is common in countries around the Swahili coast like Kenya and Tanzania; hence, it is also called Swahili pilau. Although there are a lot of Indian influences in the local cuisine of the countries around the Swahili coast, The Kenyan or Swahili pilau is more similar in appearance to the Middle Eastern version with a light brown colour than the Indian pilau with bright yellow colour from the addition of turmeric

Ingredients

  • 1 Big Onion
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp pilau masala
  • 1/2 Teaspoon salt
  • 1 garlic and 1 ginger
  • 1/4 kg boiled/fried meat
  • Oil for cooking
  • 2 cupful of rice
  • 4 cups water

Steps

  1. Heat the oil on medium heat. Put the chopped onions to the oil. Add the grated ginger and garlic and keep turning until they turn golden brown.
  2. Put the chopped tomatoes, coriander, chilli and keep mixing. Add the salt, masala to the mix.
  3. Keep adding water to avoid stickiness and to form a paste. Add the already cooked meat to the paste and allow the meat to absorb the flavours.
  4. Add the rice and mix for few seconds before pouring water.
  5. Cook for 20 minutes under medium heat and let it cool under low heat until all water runs dry.
  6. Serve with a drink or stew of your choice.
